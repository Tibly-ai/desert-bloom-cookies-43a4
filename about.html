Our Story

A little desert kitchen, a lot of heart, and a stubborn belief that snacks should actually be good for you.

How It Began

Desert Bloom started in a sun-warmed kitchen just outside Joshua Tree, where our founder was tired of choosing between "tastes good" and "does good." After years of tinkering with oat flour, desert honey, and a whole lot of trial and error, the first batch of Prickly Pear Oat cookies came out of the oven — and the neighbors wouldn't stop asking for more.

What began as trays passed over the fence turned into a folding table at the Joshua Tree Farmers Market. Every Saturday since, we've been showing up with warm cookies, cold water, and stories to share with anyone who stops by our stand.

Baked, Not Manufactured

No factory lines, no fillers, no mystery ingredients. Every batch is mixed, shaped, and baked by hand in small trays that fit in our home oven — the same way it's been done since the very first batch.

  • 🌵 Small batches, twice a week
  • 🌵 No preservatives or refined sugar
  • 🌵 Sourced within 60 miles when we can

Our Wholefood Philosophy

We believe the high desert has everything we need — you just have to know how to look. Our recipes lean on ingredients that grow, thrive, or are gathered close to home.

🌾

Whole Grains

Rolled oats and stone-ground flours in place of anything bleached or stripped of its goodness.

🍯

Desert Sweetness

Local mesquite honey and date syrup sweeten our dough — no refined sugar ever makes the cut.
